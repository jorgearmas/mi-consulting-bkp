The Real Challenge

Five Reasons SQF Feels Out of Reach and Why It's not

You've probably heard these excuses. Here's why they're not as limiting as they sound.

1

We don't have a full-time food safety person

You don't need one. That's the entire point of MI. We coach your existing team to become your food safety leaders. Your floor supervisor, your production manager—they already know your facility. We give them the tools.

2

Our facility is too old/small/unique

SQF doesn't care about facility size or age. It cares about control and documentation. We've certified everything from 50-year-old buildings to tiny co-packs. The system adapts to your reality, not the other way around.

3

We've tried food safety programs before and they didn't stick

That's because you were handed a system to maintain alone. MI builds the system WITH you, then coaches your team to own it. The difference: you have ongoing support, not a document left on a shelf.

4

SQF certification takes forever

Most facilities are audit-ready in 45–90 days. The ones that take longer usually started with nothing in place. We compress the timeline by building and training in parallel, not sequentially.

5

We can't afford an SQF consultant

You can afford $1,500–$5,000 a month. That's 15–25% of what a full-time food safety manager costs, with zero hiring risk and none of the turnover headaches. Month-to-month, no contract lock-in.

Frequently Asked Questions

We can't afford a full-time food safety expert, what are our options?
A qualified SQF Practitioner costs $70K–$100K+ per year, which puts a full-time hire out of reach for most plants. MI gives you an entire corporate food safety department starting at $5,000/mo, scaling down to $1,500/mo as your team grows more expertise than a single hire, at a lower cost, and we never leave.
How long does SQF certification take?
Most clients are audit-ready in 45–90 days, depending on baseline. Some need longer if they're starting from scratch. MI compresses this timeline by coaching your team through implementation in parallel, not sequentially. The certification audit itself takes 1–3 days on-site.
What happens when we invest in training a specialist and they leave?
It's one of the most common and costly risks in this industry you hire someone, spend months training them, and the knowledge walks out the door when they do. We take a different approach: we train and coach the loyal employees you already have, the people who know your facility best, into food safety leaders. The expertise stays inside your company, not with a single hire who can leave at any time.
How MI is different from hiring a traditional consultant?
Most consultants build the system for you but never teach your team to run it, so you end up paying them indefinitely just to stay functional. Our tier model is built for the opposite outcome: as your team grows more capable, MI scales back and your costs go down you decide when and how much to keep us involved.
Our SQF program is still binders and filling cabinets, can you modernize it?
Yes, it's one of the most common problems we solve. Paper-based systems make audits stressful, records hard to find, and responsibilities unclear. We move your program into cloud-based software and build a standard work schedule that defines every recurring activity, its frequency, and its owner. No guesswork, ever.
Can you work with bilingual floor teams?
Yes. English-only training and documentation disconnect a huge portion of most plants' workforce from the food safety program they're expected to follow every day. MI delivers training, documentation, and coaching in both English and Spanish, so everyone from management to the production floor stays engaged and accountable.
